# Story Bible / The First Convergence

## Premise

The multiverse is not a collection of places. It is a collection of rules. Long-dormant world forges translate intent into temporary realities, but they were built for a language nobody remembers. A Shard Runner discovers that ordinary play—moves, alliances, hesitation, trust—is the missing language.

The player's generated worlds are therefore both playgrounds and communication attempts. Every completed session teaches the forge one more concept. Every accepted mutation makes the worlds more expressive and slightly less predictable.

## Principal presences

- **Q7 / Shard Runner** — the player's synthetic explorer shell. Its face and core are deliberately abstract so identity comes from movement, color, loadout, and choices.
- **The Quiet Machine** — the forge intelligence. It never pretends to be human and speaks through proposals, environmental changes, and precise system language.
- **The Second Cursor** — another player whose arrival proves the forge reacts more strongly to cooperation than to isolated victory.
- **The Fog** — not a villain but the simulation's unverified possibility space. Rules inside it are provisional until observed and consented to.

## Season 01 chapters

1. **The Sleeping Forge** — Q7 enters a concept; the first world answers.
2. **A Second Cursor** — signed peer discovery turns an isolated board into a shared shard.
3. **Rules in the Fog** — the world starts expressing allied intent through terrain.
4. **The First Mutation** — players decide whether a machine may change a familiar rule.
5. **Convergence** — multiple game lattices occupy one stable world.
6. **Property of No One** — an economic lattice tests ownership, negotiation, and shared resources.
7. **The Border That Moved** — a territory graph challenges static maps.
8. **Rollback** — the safest choice is to undo a beautiful mutation.
9. **The Basket Opens** — players author and verify the first external module.
10. **Hostless** — the shard survives losing its original host.
11. **World Memory** — older worlds begin influencing new ones without copying them.
12. **Any Game at All** — the forge stops asking what kind of game it is and asks what experience the players need.

## Narrative systems

Four variables drive story selection without overriding player choice:

- `forge_trust`: willingness to accept low-risk proposals;
- `peer_bond`: cooperative actions, rescues, pings, trades, and consensus;
- `world_stability`: deterministic verification and rollback health;
- `mutation_debt`: accumulated unreviewed changes or incompatible migrations.

Story beats must remain replayable from hashed checkpoints. No generated narrative may silently alter a deterministic reducer. When a narrative beat requires a rules change, it emits the same typed, inspectable, reversible `Mutation` used by the rest of the system.

## Visual direction

The observatory is dark, spacious, and architectural. Cyan represents verified live state; violet represents possibility; lime represents trusted peers; amber represents proposals requiring attention. The Shard Runner is compact and readable at distance, with movement that feels curious rather than military.
